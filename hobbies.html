    What is a supernova?

    As the song states above, a supernova is a the explosion caused by the collapse of a giant star. Think of it as the sonic boom of a plane.

    We actually haven't taken a photo of a supernova! Most are 3-D models of what an explosion would look like.

    There is large amounts of gravity pushed upon the star, while nuclear force keeps the spherical shape of the star!

    Stars can leave behind nebulae!

    What are stars?

    Stars are big balls of gas consisting of mostly hydrogen. They use nuclear fusion to produce the light you see in the sky!

    How do stars work? They form by the collapse of a gaseous nebula comprising of majority hydrogen.

    Astronomers are able to determine aspects of the star like mass, age, metallicity, from their observations of brightness, electronmagnetic spectrum and it's movement in the sky.

    Stars can actually form a orbital star system with another star they are called binary stars!

    What are meteorites?

    They are giant rocks that produce either during planetary formation or remnants of interactions between planets. Some of them even land on earth.

    Meteorites can tell us a lot of the ways planets form by their material.

    Meteorites can be broken up into several categories based on their elemental composition.

    Some meteors happen in intervals that scientists have been able to track.
